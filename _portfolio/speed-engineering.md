Improved pressure vessel safety margin by 89% through high-fidelity FEA validation and structural optimization using ANSYS Workbench.

## Project Overview: Pressure Vessel Validation

During this internship, I focused on the structural integrity of industrial pressure vessels. These components must withstand extreme internal pressures and environmental factors, where precise simulation is required to ensure compliance with safety standards.

### Engineering Analysis Workflow
I utilized **ANSYS Workbench** to simulate the behavior of the vessel through three distinct lenses:

1. **Structural Analysis**: Evaluating the vessel's response to internal pressure and mechanical loading. This involved calculating Von-Mises stresses, identifying localized strain, and ensuring material deformation remained within elastic limits.
2. **Harmonic Analysis**: Assessing the system’s response to constant cyclic loads. By analyzing frequency response, I identified potential vibration issues to prevent resonance-induced fatigue and structural failure.
3. **Thermal Analysis**: Performing steady-state simulations to identify temperature distributions across the vessel walls and identifying localized hot spots that could compromise material integrity over time.



## Key Contributions & Results

* **FEA Validation**: Performed comprehensive validation in ANSYS, identifying critical stress concentrations in nozzle-to-shell junctions that exceeded safety thresholds under peak operating pressure.
* **Design Optimization**: Proposed and validated geometric modifications, including reinforcement pad optimizations and fillet radius adjustments to redistribute stress.
* **Safety Margin Improvement**: The final design iteration achieved an **89% improvement in the safety margin**, successfully meeting stringent industry safety factors.
* **Technical Documentation**: Authored detailed simulation reports for client delivery, documenting mesh convergence studies, boundary conditions, and final design recommendations.

## Technologies & Skills

* **Core Competencies**: Stress Linearization, Frequency Response, Failure Mode Identification
* **Industry Knowledge**: Exposure to pressure vessel design principles and safety validation.
