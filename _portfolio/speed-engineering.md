## Project Overview: Pressure Vessel Validation
During this internship, I focused on the structural integrity of industrial pressure vessels. These components operate under extreme thermal gradients and internal pressures, where even minor design flaws can lead to catastrophic failure.

### Structural & Thermal Analysis
I utilized **ANSYS Workbench** to simulate the behavior of the vessel under operational loads. This involved:
* **Steady-State Thermal Analysis**: Identifying temperature distributions and localized hot spots.
* **Static Structural Analysis**: Mapping thermal loads onto the geometry to calculate Von-Mises stresses and deformation.
* **Harmonic Analysis**: Evaluating the system's response to constant cyclic loads to prevent resonance-induced fatigue.



## Key Contributions & Results

* **FEA Validation**: Performed comprehensive validation in ANSYS, identifying critical stress concentrations in the nozzle-to-shell junctions that exceeded safety thresholds.
* **Design Optimization**: Proposed and validated geometric modifications, including reinforcement pad optimizations and fillet radius adjustments.
* **Safety Margin Improvement**: The final design iteration achieved an **89% improvement in the safety margin**, ensuring compliance with stringent safety factors.
* **Technical Documentation**: Authored detailed simulation reports for client delivery, documenting mesh convergence studies, boundary conditions, and final design recommendations.
